How to Create a Morning Routine That Sets You Up for Success

May 16, 2019 · 12 min read

How to Create a Morning Routine That Sets You Up for Success​

Ever wonder how some people seem to effortlessly glide through their day, accomplishing more and feeling less stressed? A powerful morning routine could be their secret weapon. It's not about becoming a super-human overnight; it's about intentionally crafting the first few moments of your day to set a positive tone and prime you for success.

This article will guide you through the process of building a personalized morning routine that works for you. We'll cover the benefits, explore key elements, and provide practical tips to help you create a routine you'll actually stick with.

Why Bother with a Morning Routine? The Benefits are Real!​

Before diving into the "how," let's address the "why." A well-designed morning routine offers a surprising number of benefits, impacting everything from your mood to your productivity.

  • Reduced Stress: A calm, consistent start to the day can significantly lower stress levels. Instead of rushing and reacting, you're proactively setting the pace.

  • Increased Productivity: A focused morning routine helps you prioritize tasks and tackle them with intention, leading to greater accomplishment throughout the day.

  • Improved Focus and Concentration: Activities like meditation or mindful journaling can sharpen your mental clarity and improve your ability to concentrate.

  • Enhanced Mood: Positive morning habits, such as exercise or gratitude practices, can boost your mood and create a more optimistic outlook.

  • Better Physical Health: Incorporating exercise, healthy breakfast choices, and proper hydration into your morning routine contributes to improved physical well-being.

  • Increased Self-Discipline: Sticking to a morning routine strengthens your self-discipline, which can translate to other areas of your life.

  • More Time for Yourself: A morning routine carves out dedicated time for self-care and personal growth, which can often get lost in the shuffle of a busy day.

  • Improved Sleep Quality: Consistent morning routines can help regulate your circadian rhythm, leading to better sleep patterns.

  • Greater Sense of Control: Starting your day with intention gives you a sense of control and empowerment, reducing feelings of overwhelm.

  • Achieving Goals: Incorporating goal-setting or reviewing your goals in the morning can keep you focused and motivated to work towards your aspirations.

Key Elements of a Successful Morning Routine​

There's no one-size-fits-all morning routine. The most effective routine is one that aligns with your individual needs, goals, and preferences. However, some core elements can form the foundation of a successful morning routine for most people.

  1. Hydration:

    • Why it's important: During sleep, your body becomes dehydrated. Rehydrating first thing in the morning is crucial for flushing out toxins, boosting energy levels, and supporting overall bodily functions.
    • How to implement: Drink a glass of water (or even better, water with lemon or a pinch of sea salt) as soon as you wake up.

  2. Mindful Movement:

    • Why it's important: Exercise in the morning helps to increase blood flow, boost energy levels, improve mood, and kickstart your metabolism.
    • How to implement: Engage in 15-30 minutes of exercise. This could be a brisk walk, yoga, stretching, a gym workout, or anything you enjoy. The key is to get your body moving.

  3. Nourishment (Breakfast):

    • Why it's important: Breakfast replenishes your glucose supply, which is essential for brain function and physical energy. It also helps to stabilize blood sugar levels, preventing energy crashes later in the day.
    • How to implement: Choose a balanced breakfast that includes protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates. Examples include oatmeal with berries and nuts, eggs with whole-wheat toast and avocado, or a smoothie with protein powder, fruits, and vegetables.

  4. Mindfulness or Meditation:

    • Why it's important: Meditation or mindfulness practices can reduce stress, improve focus, and cultivate a sense of calm. Even a few minutes of mindful breathing can make a significant difference.
    • How to implement: Find a quiet space and practice deep breathing exercises, guided meditation, or simply focus on your breath for 5-10 minutes. There are many free meditation apps available to guide you.

  5. Gratitude Practice:

    • Why it's important: Expressing gratitude shifts your focus to the positive aspects of your life, boosting your mood and fostering a sense of contentment.
    • How to implement: Take a few minutes to write down three things you are grateful for in a journal or simply reflect on them mentally.

  6. Goal Setting or Review:

    • Why it's important: Reviewing your goals reminds you of what you're working towards and keeps you motivated. Setting intentions for the day helps you prioritize your tasks and stay focused on your objectives.
    • How to implement: Review your long-term goals and then identify 1-3 key tasks you want to accomplish that day that will move you closer to those goals. Write them down or visualize yourself achieving them.

  7. Learning or Personal Development:

    • Why it's important: Investing in personal development in the morning sets a positive tone and can contribute to your long-term growth.
    • How to implement: Read a book, listen to a podcast, take an online course, or spend time learning a new skill for 15-30 minutes.

  8. Sunlight Exposure:

    • Why it's important: Exposure to sunlight in the morning helps regulate your circadian rhythm, boosting alertness and improving sleep quality.
    • How to implement: Open your curtains or blinds as soon as you wake up. If possible, spend some time outdoors, even if it's just for a few minutes.

Crafting Your Personalized Morning Routine: A Step-by-Step Guide​

Now that you understand the benefits and key elements, let's create a morning routine that's tailored to your needs and preferences.

Step 1: Determine Your Goals

What do you want to achieve with your morning routine? Do you want to reduce stress, increase productivity, improve your mood, or enhance your physical health? Identifying your goals will help you select the activities that will be most effective for you. Be specific! For example, instead of "be healthier," aim for "exercise for 30 minutes."

Step 2: Assess Your Current Morning

What does your typical morning look like now? Are you rushing around, hitting the snooze button multiple times, or skipping breakfast? Understanding your current habits will help you identify areas where you can make improvements. Be honest with yourself. There's no judgment here, just awareness.

Step 3: Choose Your Activities

Based on your goals and your current morning routine, select the activities you want to incorporate into your new routine. Start with a few core elements and gradually add more as you become more comfortable. Remember, it's better to start small and be consistent than to try to do too much at once and burn out.

Here are some ideas to get you started:

  • For Stress Reduction: Meditation, deep breathing exercises, yoga, journaling, listening to calming music.
  • For Increased Productivity: Reviewing your to-do list, prioritizing tasks, setting intentions, tackling your most important task first (eating the frog).
  • For Improved Mood: Gratitude practice, exercise, listening to upbeat music, spending time in nature.
  • For Enhanced Physical Health: Exercise, healthy breakfast, drinking water, stretching.
  • For Personal Growth: Reading, listening to podcasts, taking online courses, learning a new skill.

Step 4: Design Your Routine

Create a detailed plan for your morning routine, including the order of activities and the amount of time you'll spend on each. Be realistic about how much time you have available and how much you can realistically accomplish. It is best to write down a schedule so you can visualise and stick to the schedule.

Here's an example of a 60-minute morning routine:

  • 6:00 AM: Wake up, drink water (5 minutes)
  • 6:05 AM: Exercise (30 minutes)
  • 6:35 AM: Shower (10 minutes)
  • 6:45 AM: Healthy breakfast (15 minutes)
  • 7:00 AM: Review goals and prioritize tasks (10 minutes)

Step 5: Prepare the Night Before

Set yourself up for success by preparing as much as possible the night before. Lay out your clothes, pack your gym bag, prepare your breakfast ingredients, and write down your to-do list. This will save you time and energy in the morning and make it easier to stick to your routine.

Step 6: Start Small and Be Consistent

Don't try to overhaul your entire morning routine overnight. Start with one or two small changes and gradually add more as you become more comfortable. The key is to be consistent. Even if you only have 15 minutes, commit to doing something every morning.

Step 7: Track Your Progress and Make Adjustments

Keep track of your progress and make adjustments as needed. What's working well? What's not working? Are you feeling more energized, focused, and productive? If not, experiment with different activities or adjust the timing of your routine. Your morning routine should be a dynamic process that evolves as your needs and goals change.

Step 8: Be Patient and Kind to Yourself

It takes time to develop new habits. Don't get discouraged if you miss a day or two. Just get back on track as soon as possible. Be patient and kind to yourself, and remember that every small step you take is a step in the right direction.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid​

Creating a successful morning routine isn't always easy. Here are some common pitfalls to watch out for:

  • Hitting the Snooze Button: This disrupts your sleep cycle and can leave you feeling groggy and tired. Resist the urge to snooze and get out of bed as soon as your alarm goes off.
  • Checking Your Phone Immediately: This can lead to information overload and anxiety. Resist the urge to check your email or social media until you've completed your morning routine.
  • Skipping Breakfast: As mentioned earlier, breakfast is essential for energy and focus. Make time for a healthy and balanced meal.
  • Trying to Do Too Much: Don't try to cram too many activities into your morning routine. Start small and gradually add more as you become more comfortable.
  • Lack of Consistency: Consistency is key to developing new habits. Make a commitment to stick to your morning routine as much as possible, even on weekends.
  • Not Adjusting to Your Needs: Your needs will change over time. It's important to reassess your morning routine every few months and make adjustments as needed to ensure it's still serving you well.
  • Comparing Yourself to Others: Everyone's morning routine is different. Don't compare yourself to others. Focus on creating a routine that works for you.

Examples of Morning Routines​

To give you some more inspiration, here are a few examples of morning routines tailored to different lifestyles:

The Busy Professional (45 Minutes):

  • 7:00 AM: Wake up, drink water
  • 7:05 AM: 20-minute workout (yoga, HIIT, or a quick run)
  • 7:25 AM: Shower and get ready
  • 7:40 AM: Quick and healthy breakfast (smoothie or oatmeal)
  • 7:50 AM: Review to-do list and prioritize tasks

The Student (30 Minutes):

  • 7:30 AM: Wake up, drink water
  • 7:35 AM: 15 minutes of stretching or light exercise
  • 7:50 AM: Healthy breakfast
  • 8:00 AM: Review notes or study for upcoming exams

The Stay-at-Home Parent (60 Minutes):

  • 6:00 AM: Wake up before the kids
  • 6:05 AM: 10 minutes of meditation or quiet time
  • 6:15 AM: 30-minute workout (at home or at the gym)
  • 6:45 AM: Shower and get ready
  • 7:00 AM: Prepare breakfast for yourself and the kids

The Retiree (60 Minutes):

  • 7:00 AM: Wake up, drink water
  • 7:05 AM: Gentle stretching or yoga
  • 7:20 AM: Healthy breakfast
  • 7:35 AM: Read the newspaper or a book
  • 7:50 AM: Go for a walk or engage in a hobby

These are just examples, feel free to adjust them to fit your specific needs and preferences.

Tools and Resources to Help You​

There are many tools and resources available to help you create and maintain a successful morning routine. Here are a few:

  • Meditation Apps: Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer
  • Fitness Apps: Nike Training Club, Peloton, Fitbit
  • Habit Tracking Apps: Streaks, Habitica, Trello
  • Journaling Apps: Day One, Penzu
  • Productivity Apps: Todoist, Asana, Google Calendar
  • Books: "The Miracle Morning" by Hal Elrod, "Make Time" by Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky

The Long-Term Impact of a Morning Routine​

While the immediate benefits of a morning routine are noticeable – increased energy, better focus, and a more positive mood – the long-term impact can be even more profound. A consistent morning routine can contribute to:

  • Improved Overall Well-being: By prioritizing self-care and healthy habits, you're investing in your long-term physical and mental well-being.
  • Greater Success in All Areas of Life: The discipline and focus you cultivate in the morning can translate to greater success in your career, relationships, and personal pursuits.
  • Increased Resilience: A consistent routine can provide a sense of stability and control, helping you to better cope with stress and challenges.
  • A More Fulfilling Life: By intentionally designing your mornings, you're taking control of your life and creating a more meaningful and fulfilling existence.

Final Thoughts: Start Today!​

Creating a morning routine that sets you up for success is an investment in yourself. It's a commitment to prioritizing your well-being and creating a life you love. Don't be afraid to experiment, adjust, and find what works best for you. Start small, be consistent, and be patient. The rewards are well worth the effort. So, what are you waiting for? Start crafting your perfect morning routine today!
